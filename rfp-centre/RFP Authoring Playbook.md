# RFP Authoring Playbook

Internal working note for maintaining the nine Product Research Initiative RFP pages. Public submission rules live in [[How to Apply]], [[Submission Pack]], and [[Evaluation Criteria]].

## Current Publication Model

Each RFP has two layers:

1. A short website page in `rfp-centre/`.
2. A full tender specification in `rfp-full-draft/`.

The website page is for public scanning and bidder orientation. The full specification carries detailed decision gates, methodology guidance, deliverables, acceptance criteria, governance, and appendices.

## Standard Website Page Shape

Each public RFP page should include:

```text
# RFP NN - Title

Full tender specification: [link]

## At a Glance
## Evidence Gap
## Scope
## Methodology Bar
## Expected Decision Outputs
## Evaluation Note
## Shared Process
```

Do not repeat shared submission, evaluation, clarification, or data-handling boilerplate on the public RFP pages. Link to the shared process pages instead.

## Quality Gate

Before an RFP page is marked publication-ready, check:

- strategic question is clear in the At a Glance table;
- evidence gap is specific and decision-oriented;
- scope has in-scope and out-of-scope boundaries;
- methodology bar names the evidence needed;
- decision outputs are concrete;
- full tender specification link exists;
- page points to [[How to Apply]], [[Submission Pack]], [[Evaluation Criteria]], and [[Clarifications & FAQ]];
- no outdated references to the earlier twelve-RFP working slate;
- feedback has been logged in [[Feedback Incorporation Log]].

## Full Specification Trimming Rules

The full specs should remain procurement-grade, but avoid unnecessary repetition.

Keep:

- executive summary;
- decision gates;
- scope boundaries;
- methodology bar;
- deliverables and acceptance criteria;
- RFP-specific proposal requirements;
- RFP-specific evaluation guidance;
- governance, confidentiality, conflicts, and terms where needed.

Move or reference:

- shared evaluation rubric;
- shared submission pack;
- repeated budget-format instructions;
- website/design handoff notes;
- generic clarification process text.

## Cross-RFP Coordination

Several RFPs share respondent groups:

| Shared population | RFPs that touch it |
|---|---|
| Current and potential Cardano users/builders | RFP 01, RFP 02, RFP 04, RFP 07 |
| Enterprise buyers/operators | RFP 02, RFP 06, RFP 08, RFP 09 |
| Stablecoin issuers, liquidity providers, market makers | RFP 03, RFP 05, RFP 06, RFP 07 |
| Delivery partners and system integrators | RFP 05, RFP 06, RFP 08, RFP 09 |
| AI builders, buyers, and agent-payment actors | RFP 09, with handoffs to RFP 04, RFP 06, RFP 07, and RFP 08 |

Applicants bidding on multiple RFPs should disclose shared respondent plans so CPC can spot capacity assumptions and respondent-fatigue risk during evaluation.
