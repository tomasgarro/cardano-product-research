# How to Apply

This page explains the common application process for the nine Product Research Initiative RFPs. The Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page will state the publication date, submission deadline, clarification window, budget, and submission channel.

## Who Can Apply

The RFPs are open to individuals, research teams, consultancies, academic groups, ecosystem teams, commercial entities, and consortia that can meet the relevant evidence standard.

Applicants may submit to more than one RFP. If they do, they should disclose:

- shared staffing assumptions;
- shared respondent recruitment;
- shared data collection;
- capacity constraints;
- any dependency between proposals.

Subcontracting is permitted where roles, responsibilities, costs, and conflicts are disclosed.

## Application Steps

1. Read the relevant RFP summary page.
2. Read the full tender specification linked from that page.
3. Review [[Evaluation Criteria]] and [[Submission Pack]].
4. Submit clarification questions through the published clarification process if needed.
5. Prepare the required proposal documents.
6. Submit through the official channel named on the Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page.

Informal messages to committee members, vendors, or contributors do not count as submission and should not be relied on for guidance.

## Timeline Shape

Exact dates will be stated on the Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page. The usual process is:

| Phase | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Publication | RFPs and shared process pages go live |
| Clarification window | Applicants submit questions; official answers are published for all |
| Submission deadline | Complete proposals are due |
| Eligibility check | Submissions are checked for completeness and conflicts |
| Evaluation | Eligible proposals are scored using [[Evaluation Criteria]] |
| Award decision | CPC awards, requests revision, or declines to award |
| Kickoff | Selected vendor aligns scope, workplan, data handling, and reporting cadence |

Late submissions are not accepted unless the CPC issues a written extension through the public process.

## Budget Guidance

Portfolio budget information belongs on the Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page. Individual proposals should include the budget required to meet the evidence standard for the selected RFP.

Budgets may be submitted in ADA or another currency where the ADA-equivalent value and conversion basis are shown.

Cost is evaluated for value for money, not lowest price. Applicants should explain how the requested budget maps to scope, respondent access, data costs, specialist expertise, deliverables, and decision value.

## Clarifications

Applicants should submit clarification questions through the process described in [[Clarifications & FAQ]]. Material answers are published for all applicants.

Questions should not seek private advantage. If a question affects scope, eligibility, timing, deliverables, or evaluation, the answer must be public.

## After Award

Awarded vendors will align with CPC or the designated review group before fieldwork begins. At minimum, kickoff should confirm:

- decision gates;
- methodology and sample plan;
- workplan and milestones;
- data handling and confidentiality;
- public and confidential outputs;
- reporting cadence;
- final publication expectations.

Awarded vendors should use [[Research Insights Documentation Framework]] for final outputs unless a specific RFP states otherwise.
