## Evidence Gap

Cardano has a growing stablecoin stack, but liquidity depth remains thin across major assets and venues. The question is not simply whether more liquidity would help. The question is what kind of liquidity is missing, which users need it, where it should exist, and how funding can avoid paying for short-term mercenary capital.

This RFP should define what Cardano should pay for, how it should measure success, and when it should withhold or stop funding.

## Scope

In scope:

- stablecoin liquidity baseline across major assets and venues;
- depth, slippage, spread, route, pool, and venue analysis;
- DeFi, CEX, market-maker, issuer, bridge, wallet, and payment access constraints where relevant;
- demand-side validation for priority use cases;
- incentive model comparison;
- monitoring and anti-gaming framework;
- staged activation roadmap.

Out of scope:

- operating the liquidity incentive programme;
- paying liquidity providers, market makers, exchanges, or protocols;
- full customer segmentation;
- full regional entry or enterprise readiness strategy.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should not rely on TVL or volume alone. They should explain how they will measure usable depth, slippage, spread, route quality, retention, and demand. Proprietary or commercially sensitive data may be used, but access conditions and publication limits must be clear.

Strong proposals will include demand validation from users or operators who need stablecoin liquidity at defined sizes, assets, and venues.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Stablecoin liquidity baseline.
- Asset and venue gap analysis.
- Demand-side validation memo.
- Incentive model comparison.
- Liquidity incentive scorecard.
- Monitoring and anti-gaming framework.
- 6, 12, and 24-month roadmap with funding triggers and stop conditions.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal can measure current conditions, diagnose bottlenecks by asset and venue, validate demand, compare incentive models, and recommend measurable funding triggers.

Common weak points include assuming the answer is "more liquidity," treating TVL as sufficient, ignoring market-maker or exchange constraints, and omitting anti-gaming safeguards.
