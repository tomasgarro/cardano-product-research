# Submission Pack

Every bid should include the documents below unless the specific RFP states otherwise. The goal is to make proposals comparable while giving applicants room to explain their method.

## Required Documents

| # | Document | Purpose | Suggested length |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover letter | Applicant identity, RFP reference, contact, budget total, timeline, declarations | 1 page |
| 2 | Technical proposal | Understanding of the brief, methodology, decision-gate mapping, workplan, deliverables | Up to 15 pages |
| 3 | Budget proposal | Line-item budget, assumptions, optional stretch scope, ADA-equivalent basis | 1-3 pages or spreadsheet |
| 4 | Team credentials | Team roles, relevant experience, prior work, subcontractors | Concise CVs or bios |
| 5 | Evidence access plan | Respondent/data access, recruitment logic, data sources, confidence limits | 1-2 pages |
| 6 | Ethics and data handling statement | Consent, anonymisation, confidentiality, retention, publication boundaries | 1-2 pages |
| 7 | Conflicts declaration | Actual, potential, or perceived conflicts and mitigation | Included in cover letter or separate |

## Technical Proposal Contents

The technical proposal should cover:

- understanding of the strategic question and evidence gap;
- proposed methodology;
- decision-gate mapping;
- sample, respondent, or data access plan;
- workplan and milestones;
- deliverables approach;
- risks, limitations, and bias controls;
- public/confidential output treatment;
- budget logic summary;
- dependencies on CPC, Intersect, respondents, proprietary data, or third parties.

Long CVs, raw instruments, detailed data schemas, and prior work examples should sit in appendices rather than the main proposal.

## Budget Proposal Contents

Budgets should separate:

- personnel;
- research operations;
- respondent incentives;
- data, tools, or paid sources;
- travel, if any;
- subcontractors;
- project management;
- overhead;
- optional stretch scope;
- total budget request.

Applicants should state the currency, ADA-equivalent value, conversion basis, and assumptions that could change pricing.

## Evidence Access Plan

The evidence access plan should explain:

- who will be interviewed, surveyed, benchmarked, or otherwise studied;
- why those sources are relevant;
- what access is confirmed versus assumed;
- what fallback plan exists if access fails;
- what evidence can be published, inspected confidentially, or only summarised;
- how the team will avoid overclaiming from thin or biased evidence.

## Ethics and Data Handling

Any proposal involving interviews, surveys, or commercially sensitive data should explain:

- respondent consent;
- attribution and anonymisation choices;
- recording and transcript handling;
- storage and access controls;
- retention and deletion;
- public summary boundaries;
- treatment of confidential, proprietary, or commercially sensitive information.

## File and Format Expectations

Final submission format will be confirmed on the Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page. Preferred formats:

- PDF for formal documents;
- spreadsheet or table for budgets;
- Markdown or machine-readable copy where practical;
- clear filenames that include the RFP number and applicant name.
