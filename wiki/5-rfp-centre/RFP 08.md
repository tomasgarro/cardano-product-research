# RFP 08 - Enterprise Delivery Partner Network

## Evidence Gap

Enterprise adoption often requires accountable delivery partners who can scope, implement, integrate, operate, support, and maintain solutions through procurement and production use. Cardano does not yet have a decision-ready map of which firms can do this today, which could become ready, and what certification or partner programme would be credible.

This RFP should determine what type of partner network would be useful rather than symbolic.

## Scope

In scope:

- current and potential Cardano delivery partner landscape mapping;
- enterprise delivery capability taxonomy;
- certification framework and partner-tier recommendation;
- peer ecosystem partner-network benchmarking;
- co-funded implementation model assessment;
- larger-consultancy and system-integrator barrier analysis;
- geography and sector gap analysis;
- training, enablement, governance, and quality-control requirements;
- pilot engagement pathway and evidence thresholds.

Out of scope:

- operating the partner programme;
- implementation delivery;
- enterprise sales execution;
- legal advice;
- full enterprise/RWA readiness assessment.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should screen partner categories, regions, sectors, peer models, and candidate certification structures before deep dives. Strong proposals will include delivery-firm, buyer/operator, SI, or peer-program evidence where feasible.

Certification criteria should be tied to real delivery capability, not logos, community visibility, or unverifiable partnership claims.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Partner landscape map.
- Enterprise delivery capability model.
- Certification framework recommendation.
- Peer reference-model benchmark.
- Co-funded implementation guardrails.
- Partner recruitment and go-to-market model.
- Governance and quality-control model.
- Pilot engagement pathway.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal can explain what delivery capability enterprise buyers need, which partner types can provide it, and what certification or co-funding model would create real adoption outcomes.

Common weak points include symbolic certification, generic partnership strategy, weak firm access, or recommendations that subsidise consulting activity without adoption thresholds.
