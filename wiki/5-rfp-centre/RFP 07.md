# RFP 07 - L2 Adoption and Interoperability Demand Study

Full tender specification: [RFP-07-L2-Adoption-and-Interoperability-Demand-Study.md](../../rfq/RFP-07-L2-Adoption-and-Interoperability-Demand-Study.md)

## Evidence Gap

Cardano has credible signs of demand for L2 infrastructure and interoperability, but lacks a decision-ready map of what is actually blocked, which capabilities are required, and which cross-chain pathways create net-new or mutual value rather than dependency or value leakage.

This RFP is not a technical architecture selection. It is a demand and prioritisation study.

## Scope

In scope:

- L2 blocker analysis;
- L2 demand mapping;
- application and use-case screening;
- interoperability requirements mapping;
- bridge, partner-chain, wallet, and infrastructure provider analysis;
- value-flow assessment;
- negative-case and non-Cardano evidence;
- investment sequencing and evidence thresholds.

Out of scope:

- designing or selecting a full L2 protocol architecture;
- formal bridge security audit;
- full developer tooling audit;
- stablecoin incentive design;
- full enterprise, government, delivery partner, or use-case strategy.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should use a screening-first design and explain which applications, blockers, provider opportunities, corridors, or interoperability features deserve deeper analysis.

Strong proposals will separate technical blockers from commercial, liquidity, UX, documentation, wallet, partner, or coordination blockers. They should include relevant builder/operator evidence and provider-side evidence where possible.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- L2 barrier analysis.
- L2 demand map with deployment conditions and confidence levels.
- Interoperability requirements register.
- Interoperability value-flow assessment.
- Provider and partner opportunity analysis.
- Investment sequencing recommendation.
- Evidence threshold framework for future proposals.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal can show which infrastructure work is pulled by validated adoption demand and which interoperability pathways are value-accretive, mutual-value, neutral-access, dependency-risk, or value-leakage risk.

Common weak points include abstract technical advocacy, feature wish lists without deployment evidence, and treating interoperability as automatically good for Cardano.
