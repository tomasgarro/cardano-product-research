# RFP 04 - Brand Awareness and Perception

Full tender specification: [RFP-04-Brand-Awareness-and-Perception.md](../../rfq/RFP-04-Brand-Awareness-and-Perception.md)

## At a Glance

| Item | Summary |
|---|---|
| Strategic question | How do external audiences perceive Cardano, and which evidence-backed positioning moves would improve credibility? |
| Core decision | Which audiences, messages, proof assets, and brand associations should Cardano prioritise? |
| Evidence bar | Audience-segmented perception research with competitive comparison and proof-requirement mapping |
| Main outputs | Perception baseline, audience evaluation map, positioning gap analysis, message/proof tests, positioning playbook |
| Budget and dates | See the Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page |

## Evidence Gap

Cardano lacks a clear baseline on how different external audiences evaluate it as a place to build, invest, partner, deploy, or recommend. Awareness, perception, credibility, and consideration are different questions and should not be collapsed into generic sentiment.

This RFP asks what each audience believes, what it compares Cardano against, what objections block consideration, and what proof would make a claim credible.

## Scope

In scope:

- awareness, perception, credibility, and consideration by audience;
- competitor perception against Solana, Ethereum L2s, and justified peers;
- narrative gap analysis;
- audience proof requirements and objections;
- message and use-case framing tests;
- brand association or partnership-category assessment;
- perception tracking framework.

Out of scope:

- campaign execution;
- media buying;
- brand redesign;
- full customer segmentation;
- full technical competitor analysis;
- full enterprise, government, stablecoin, L2, or AI research.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should justify audience coverage and sample design. A broad general-public sample is optional and should be justified; targeted external audiences such as builders, enterprises, analysts, institutions, partners, and crypto-aware users are likely more decision-useful.

Strong proposals will distinguish messaging problems from substantive product, liquidity, delivery, enterprise, or ecosystem blockers.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Awareness and perception baseline.
- Competitive perception benchmark.
- Audience evaluation map.
- Positioning gap analysis.
- Objection and proof requirement register.
- Message and use-case framing test results.
- Positioning playbook and tracking framework.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal shows how perception evidence will support concrete decisions about priority audiences, proof assets, messaging, use-case framings, and handoffs to non-brand workstreams.

Common weak points include generic sentiment analysis, internal-only feedback, campaign recommendations without evidence, and treating real product blockers as messaging issues.
