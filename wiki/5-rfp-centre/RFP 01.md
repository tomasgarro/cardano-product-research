# RFP 01 - Customer Segmentation and Profiling

Full tender specification: [RFP-01-Customer-Segmentation-and-Profiling.md](../../rfq/RFP-01-Customer-Segmentation-and-Profiling.md)

## At a Glance

| Item | Summary |
|---|---|
| Strategic question | Who are Cardano's current and potential customer segments, what does each need, and which should be prioritised for activation? |
| Core decision | Which segments should Cardano stakeholders prioritise first, through which channels and partners? |
| Evidence bar | Mixed-method segmentation research with current Cardano participants and external non-Cardano respondents |
| Main outputs | Segment map, priority segment briefs, channel matrix, adoption signal framework, decision memo |
| Budget and dates | See the Product Research Initiatives - Grants main page |

## Evidence Gap

Cardano does not yet have a validated segmentation model that distinguishes current users, builders, operators, partners, and potential external demand segments. Without this, product strategy, funding, communications, and partner development risk being too broad to produce measurable adoption.

This RFP asks vendors to move beyond generic labels such as "developers," "retail," "enterprise," or "institutions." Segments must be based on behaviour, jobs-to-be-done, decision criteria, current alternatives, adoption blockers, switching conditions, and reachable channels.

## Scope

In scope:

- current Cardano customer and user profiling;
- potential external demand segments beyond the crypto-native audience;
- jobs-to-be-done, decision criteria, blockers, alternatives, and switching conditions;
- segment prioritisation for the next 12-24 months;
- partner and channel routes by segment;
- adoption signal definitions by segment.

Out of scope:

- full enterprise readiness assessment;
- full brand perception study;
- full use-case landscape or competitive audit;
- stablecoin liquidity design;
- L2/interoperability technical research;
- campaign execution or product roadmap ownership.

Findings that belong to another RFP should be captured in a cross-RFP handoff memo.

## Methodology Bar

Desk research alone is not sufficient. Applicants should propose primary validation with current Cardano users, builders, operators, ecosystem stakeholders, external non-Cardano respondents, and partner or channel candidates where relevant.

Strong proposals will explain how they will avoid insider bias, include negative or rejected cases where possible, and distinguish stated interest from real adoption signal.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Validated segment map with evidence basis and confidence level.
- Segment prioritisation model showing top, watchlist, and deprioritised segments.
- Segment briefs covering job-to-be-done, blocker, alternative, switching condition, value proposition, and channel route.
- Segment x vertical x channel matrix.
- Adoption signal framework separating strong indicators from cosmetic activity.
- Executive decision memo and public summary.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal maps methods and deliverables to the segmentation decision gates, includes external validation beyond Cardano insiders, and shows how the final segmentation will guide prioritisation, partner strategy, and adoption measurement.

Common weak points include generic personas, desk-only segmentation, broad audience labels without behavioural evidence, and all-positive recommendations.
