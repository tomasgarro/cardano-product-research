# RFP 02 - Use-Case Landscape and Competitive Positioning

## Evidence Gap

Cardano has visible projects and strong vertical narratives, but it lacks an operator-grounded view of which use cases are live and growing, flat, dormant, failed, or unproven. It also lacks a practical benchmark showing where operators choose Cardano, reject it, leave it, or choose peer ecosystems instead.

This RFP is designed to replace narrative-based vertical prioritisation with evidence about real adoption signal and competitive advantage.

## Scope

In scope:

- Cardano use-case inventory and maturity classification;
- vertical screening and justified deep-dive selection;
- competitive benchmarking by selected vertical;
- operator win/loss research;
- adoption signal assessment;
- attack, partner, wait, stop, or handoff recommendations.

Out of scope:

- full customer segmentation;
- full stablecoin incentive design;
- full enterprise readiness assessment;
- regional government-entry playbooks;
- full L2/interoperability demand study;
- broad brand perception research.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should propose a screening-first design: map the broader landscape, then deep dive only where evidence access and decision value justify it.

Primary research with operators is required. Strong proposals should include Cardano operators, non-Cardano operators, negative cases, and cross-ecosystem experts. The full specification asks for a win/loss register of at least 15 operator decisions, with confidential attribution to CPC allowed where public naming is not possible.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Use-case map classified by maturity and evidence quality.
- Vertical screening matrix and deep-dive rationale.
- Competitive benchmark by vertical.
- Operator win/loss register.
- Adoption signal framework by vertical.
- Vertical wedge analysis: attack, partner, wait, stop, or hand off.
- Executive decision memo and public summary.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal can explain where Cardano has real traction, where it loses, why operators make those decisions, and which verticals deserve priority over the next 24 months.

Common weak points include generic blockchain landscapes, project lists without adoption status, Cardano-only interviews, competitor narratives without operator evidence, and recommendations that are all-positive.
