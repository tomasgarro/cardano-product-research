## Evidence Gap

Government and emerging-market adoption cannot be treated as one global opportunity. LATAM, Africa, East Asia, and other candidate regions have different use cases, regulatory dependencies, partner pathways, infrastructure constraints, languages, and delivery needs.

This RFP should identify which region/use-case plays are credible on a 12-24 month horizon and which should be monitored, deferred, or avoided.

## Scope

In scope:

- regional market prioritisation;
- use-case viability by region;
- public-sector, humanitarian, multilateral, commercial, academic, and local partner pathways;
- regulatory landscape summaries sufficient for go/no-go or further diligence decisions;
- infrastructure prerequisites such as stablecoins, off-ramps, identity, privacy, mobile access, interoperability, and delivery capacity;
- adoption signal framework and cosmetic-activity filter.

Out of scope:

- lobbying or public affairs execution;
- direct government representation;
- legal advice;
- pilot execution;
- full stablecoin, enterprise, delivery partner, L2, brand, or customer segmentation research.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should use a screening-first approach and avoid treating continents as single markets. Strong proposals will justify country clusters, use-case selection, counterparty access, and local/regional expertise.

Regulatory analysis should identify dependencies and uncertainty; it should not present legal conclusions unless separately qualified.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Market prioritisation matrix.
- Regional entry playbooks.
- Regulatory landscape summaries.
- Counterparty and partner access map.
- Use-case viability assessment.
- Infrastructure prerequisite assessment.
- Research-to-action roadmap with go, conditional-go, monitor, or no-action recommendations.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal can move from broad regional narratives to specific entry plays with reachable counterparties, clear dependencies, measurable adoption signals, and realistic next actions.

Common weak points include broad region lists, event-based adoption claims, unsupported government-interest claims, and legal or regulatory overclaiming.
