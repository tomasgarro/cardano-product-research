# RFP 06 - Enterprise and RWA Readiness Assessment

## Evidence Gap

Enterprise and RWA adoption depend on more than protocol capability. Buyers, issuers, operators, custodians, delivery partners, and regulated-market participants may require ROI evidence, compliance documentation, custody and settlement pathways, support expectations, procurement fit, delivery ownership, and risk controls.

This RFP should identify where production deployment is plausible, what blocks conversion, and which readiness gaps should be funded or coordinated first.

## Scope

In scope:

- enterprise production-readiness assessment;
- enterprise vertical prioritisation;
- RWA category assessment;
- buyer, issuer, operator, custodian, and delivery requirement mapping;
- pilot-to-production conversion analysis;
- compliance-readiness and diligence mapping;
- support, SLA, custody, settlement, integration, and infrastructure dependency assessment;
- adoption signal framework and gap closure plan.

Out of scope:

- enterprise sales execution;
- legal advice;
- token promotion;
- implementation delivery;
- full delivery partner certification design;
- generic RWA market reporting.

Delivery partner network findings should be handed to [[RFP 08]] rather than fully solved here.

## Methodology Bar

Applicants should screen possible verticals and RWA categories before choosing deep dives. Strong proposals will include external buyer, operator, institutional, compliance, or delivery stakeholder evidence where feasible.

Compliance-readiness mapping should identify questions, documentation needs, and diligence triggers without overclaiming legal feasibility.

## Expected Decision Outputs

- Enterprise readiness scorecard.
- Enterprise vertical prioritisation matrix.
- RWA category assessment.
- Buyer requirement map.
- Pilot-to-production conversion framework.
- Compliance-readiness and infrastructure dependency maps.
- Gap closure plan and adoption signal framework.

## Evaluation Note

Strong fit means the proposal can distinguish technical possibility, proof of concept, pilot, production deployment, recurring usage, and durable value.

Common weak points include broad RWA narratives, treating announcements as adoption, ignoring buyer requirements, or duplicating the delivery partner network RFP.
