# Research Grants - Evaluation Framework and Process

All nine Product Research Initiative RFPs use the CPC standard research grant evaluation framework. RFP-specific pages may add guidance on what strong fit means for that initiative, but they do not replace the core framework below.

## Core Evaluation Framework

### F - Fit to Grant Objectives (0-5)

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Fully meets minimum requirements + fully covers nice-to-haves + adds meaningful extra value |
| 4 | Fully meets minimum requirements + covers most nice-to-haves |
| 3 | Fully meets minimum requirements |
| 2 | Meets most minimum requirements but with gaps |
| 1 | Major gaps in minimum requirements |
| 0 | Does not meet minimum requirements (automatic disqualification) |

### C - Cost Efficiency (0-5)

Question: Given scope and quality, how efficient is this proposal compared to the others?

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Excellent value for money |
| 4 | Strong value |
| 3 | Fair / reasonable |
| 2 | Weak value |
| 1 | Poor value |
| 0 | Unjustifiably expensive |

This avoids over-optimizing for the cheapest proposal, which is important for research grants.

### T - Team Capability (0-5)

Question: Can this team deliver?

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Proven team with directly relevant expertise + past delivery evidence |
| 4 | Strong team with relevant experience |
| 3 | Competent team, skills present but limited proof |
| 2 | Some skill gaps |
| 1 | Major skill gaps |
| 0 | No credible delivery capacity |

### P - Proposal Quality and Execution Plan (0-5)

Question: Is the execution credible?

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Extremely clear proposal, strong logic, clear milestones, timeline, risks identified |
| 4 | Clear proposal + milestones + timeline |
| 3 | Clear proposal + milestones |
| 2 | Some clarity but missing structure |
| 1 | Unclear or poorly structured |
| 0 | Incoherent |

## Weighting

| Category | Weight |
|---|---:|
| F - Fit | 40% |
| T - Team | 25% |
| P - Proposal | 20% |
| C - Cost | 15% |

Why:

- Fit matters most because this is strategic alignment.
- Team comes next because research quality depends on credible delivery.
- Proposal clarity is third because execution needs to be understandable and testable.
- Cost is last because research should not be optimized like commodity development work.

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1 - Independent Scoring

Each evaluator scores each proposal privately.

### Step 2 - Calculate Weighted Final Score

Final score per proposal:

```text
(F x 0.4) + (T x 0.25) + (P x 0.2) + (C x 0.15)
```

### Step 3 - Group Calibration

The group reviews anonymized averages from the independent scoring step.

Only the following should be discussed:

- proposals with high variance, defined as standard deviation greater than 1;
- proposals close in final ranking, defined as weighted score difference below 0.3;
- disqualification or deprioritization concerns.

One rescoring round may be allowed if needed.

### Step 4 - Final Decision

The CPC makes the final award decision after scoring, calibration, and any required rescoring.

Each evaluator fills:

| Proposal | F (0-5) | T (0-5) | P (0-5) | C (0-5) | Notes | Individual evaluator proposal score |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---:|
| [Proposal] | [Score] | [Score] | [Score] | [Score] | [Notes] | [Calculated score] |

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| [Proposal] | [Avg] | [Avg] | [Avg] | [Avg] | [Score] | [Rank] |

## RFP-Specific Guidance

Each RFP may include a short note explaining what strong fit means for that research area. Those notes should be read together with this shared framework.
